About Me

My name is Andrés Felipe Torres Martinez, I am an electronics engineer from the Pontifical Bolivarian University. My areas of interest are embedded development, electronic design, and digital design. My main software programs are Altium, KiCad, and Proteus, in which I have developed multi-layer PCBs, some of which have included RF components, so I have also worked with High-Speed ​​PCB design. I also have expertise in firmware development in C/C++ and MicroPython with chip families from ST, Texas Instruments, NXP, ESP-32, and Arduino. This includes register management, dynamic memory management, and RTOS scheduling, as well as board debugging using oscilloscopes and logic analyzers. Additionally, I have some experience working in FPGA environments, mainly creating testbenches for Sipeed and Altera chips. My projects and products in the industry include test benches for cryogenic electronics testing, IoT communication modules using Bluetooth and LoRa, motor speed rectifiers, FPGA test benches, and communication modules using Ethernet and MQTT, among others. Please feel free to contact me with any questions regarding my skills.
